# Miami Stories DOJ - Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to the official Miami Stories Department of Justice FAQ for Miami, Florida roleplay.

This FAQ explains how the Miami Stories DOJ portal supports service requests, court coordination, public docket notices, Bar licensing, warrants, subpoenas, transcripts, and staff workflows. The portal is adapted for Miami Stories RP and is not real-world legal advice.

DOJ Structure and Leadership

Who oversees the Miami Stories DOJ?

The DOJ is organized around judicial leadership, prosecution, defense counsel, Bar licensing, clerks, trainers, and support roles. Portal permissions are derived from configured Discord roles, so Miami role IDs must be set in D1 or environment configuration before production use.

What legal framework does Miami Stories use?

Miami Stories uses a Florida-inspired RP legal framework adapted for server mechanics and semi-serious roleplay. Real Florida concepts may inspire the structure, but the portal should use simplified Miami Stories legal code and procedures rather than real statutes copied word-for-word.

What are the main DOJ branches?

- Judicial Branch: Chief Justice, justices, judges, magistrate judges, clerks, court scheduling, docket publication, warrants, subpoenas, and judicial review.
- Executive / Prosecution: Attorney General, District Attorney, prosecutors, charging review, warrant support, and public safety coordination.
- Defense Branch: public defenders, private practitioners, defense attorneys, defense paralegals, and counsel access.
- Bar / Licensing: Bar Association members, Bar licensed attorneys, Bar candidates, trainers, reviewers, and professional standards.

Lawyers and Representation - Licensing and Certification

How do I request defense counsel?

Use the defense counsel route at [/services/lawyer](/services/lawyer). Include your character name, Citizen ID, custody status, charges or reason for detention, urgency, and contact method. Private details are routed to protected DOJ workflows.

How do I become licensed to practice law?

Review the official resources, take the Miami Stories Bar Examination through the portal, complete any interview or competency check required by staff, and receive the configured Bar role when approved.

What does the Bar Examination test?

The Bar Examination tests the Miami Stories legal code, court procedure, ethical conduct, professional communication, evidence handling, rights advisement, and RP courtroom standards. The exam is locked to the logged-in Discord account and uses the portal's native timer and version assignment.

Arrests Rights and Pleas

How are court hearing requests started?

Court hearing requests should include the defendant, Citizen ID if known, arrest or report number, alleged charges, arresting agency or officer, discovery notes, and scheduling notes. Staff can convert eligible service requests into docket entries.

When should probable cause or custody be reviewed?

Probable cause review, custody advisory, arraignment, preliminary hearing, warrant review, and related proceedings should be used when detention, charging, evidence, or judicial oversight needs a structured record.

What should law enforcement and prosecutors avoid?

Avoid unsupported charge stacking, vague probable cause, missing evidence links, public disclosure of private case details, and attempts to bypass counsel or judicial review where portal procedure requires it.

Civil Cases and Protective Orders

What should a civil claim include?

A civil claim should identify the plaintiff, defendant, claim type, facts, date of harm, requested relief, witnesses, exhibits, and any completed filing template. Civil claim details should stay in the protected request workflow until intentionally published.

Can administrative review be requested?

Yes. Use the available DOJ service route for general or administrative review when a matter does not fit another service type. Staff can classify and route it internally.

Subpoenas and Evidence Requests

What information is needed for a subpoena?

A subpoena request should identify the person, agency, business, records, testimony, digital evidence, or objects requested. It should explain relevance, necessity, scope, timing, and supporting facts.

Can subpoena details be posted publicly?

No. Subpoena details may include private evidence, investigative information, or attorney work product. Use the portal request form and protected ticket workflow.

Arrest and Search Warrants

Who reviews warrant requests?

Authorized judicial and prosecutorial personnel review warrant requests under Miami Stories DOJ procedure, probable cause standards, RP rights protections, and server mechanics.

What should a warrant request include?

Include the target, case number, requesting officer or agency, exact charges or property sought, probable cause facts, evidence links where available, and a clear explanation of why the requested action is limited and necessary.

How does search and seizure review work?

Search and seizure review focuses on the target, scope, probable cause, evidence requested, timing, and whether the request is specific enough for a judge to approve or deny in a clean RP-friendly way.

Evidence Bodycams and CCTV

Should private evidence be posted publicly?

No. Do not include confidential case information, private evidence, attorney-client communications, private ticket transcripts, or active investigative details in public fields.

What evidence should be preserved?

Relevant bodycam, CCTV, photos, messages, reports, witness statements, logs, attachments, and Discord ticket records should be preserved through the appropriate protected workflow.

Expungements Marriage and Other Services

What public services are available?

The portal supports defense counsel requests, court hearing requests, civil claims, subpoenas, warrant review, search and seizure review, expungements, marriage certificate review, divorce review, administrative review, and Bar Examination access.

Are expungements guaranteed?

No. Expungement is discretionary in RP and depends on the Miami Stories legal code, staff review, court order requirements, server policy, and the evidence submitted.

Can marriage and divorce records be handled by the DOJ?

Yes, if the server supports those services. Use the marriage certificate review or divorce review route and provide the required character information, record details, and contact method.

Judicial Review and Accountability

How does the public docket work?

The public docket lists scheduled hearings, judicial reviews, court notices, and published proceedings from the Miami Stories DOJ. Private notes and protected ticket details must not be exposed on public docket pages.

Who can see private ticket transcripts?

Only authorized staff may access protected transcript endpoints and the Ticket Transcripts dashboard. Public users cannot access private ticket transcript records.

How does the portal support accountability?

The portal supports accountability through public resources, public docket publication, private request history, protected transcript archives, audit events, judge assignment records, and role-gated dashboard tools.
